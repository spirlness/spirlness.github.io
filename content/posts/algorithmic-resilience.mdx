# Introduction

In recent years, the integration of physical principles into neural network design has yielded significant improvements in robustness and generalization. We refer to this property as **Algorithmic Resilience**.

<SideNote>
Algorithmic Resilience is defined here as the ability of a model to maintain performance under out-of-distribution shifts that respect the underlying physical symmetries.
</SideNote>

# Theoretical Framework

Consider a system governed by the following differential equation:

<MathBlock equation="\frac{d\mathbf{x}}{dt} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x}, t) + \sigma(\mathbf{x}) \cdot \mathbf{w}(t)" label="1" />

Where $\mathbf{w}(t)$ represents stochastic noise. In a traditional neural network, this noise can lead to catastrophic failure. However, by imposing Hamiltonian constraints:

<MathBlock equation="H(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{p}) = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{p}^T \mathbf{M}^{-1} \mathbf{p} + V(\mathbf{q})" label="2" />

We can ensure that the total energy is conserved, which significantly stabilizes the learning process.

# Conclusion

By embedding physical priors into our architectures, we don't just achieve better metrics; we gain a structural guarantee of stability.
